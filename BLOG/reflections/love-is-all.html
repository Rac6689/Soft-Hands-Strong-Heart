Love Is All You Need

I’ve always been a romantic at heart. When my partner was diagnosed, there wasn’t really a choice for me—love became my compass. It has guided me through every stage of this journey. At times, that compass has led me into difficult places filled with grief, exhaustion, and heartbreak. But even in the hardest seasons, I wouldn’t change a thing. Love has given meaning to each moment.

I also know that not every caregiving journey feels this way. Relationships can be complicated. For some, stepping into the role of caregiver is tangled with old hurts, unspoken tensions, or a love that looks very different from my own experience. I want to honor that truth, too. There are many paths through caregiving, and each is valid. This is simply mine.

What caregiving has taught me, beyond my devotion to my partner, is that I matter too. For a long time I believed love meant pouring myself out completely. But little by little, I’ve discovered that caring for myself is also an act of love—both for me and for him. Love, I’ve learned, isn’t about erasing myself for another. It’s about allowing love to flow through me in a way that includes me, too.

Beyond the personal, caregiving has opened me to a wider sense of love—the kind that extends to all beings. The kind that reminds me we are connected, fragile, and human together. In quiet moments I feel that this is perhaps the very purpose of life: to learn how to love and to let ourselves be loved.

And then there are the miracles. Some days I am not sure if my partner even recognizes me. Yet I will look up and see his face watching me, full of love, admiration, and sweetness. In those moments, whether he remembers my name or not doesn’t matter. I can feel his love for me, and my love for him, alive in the space between us. It is something deeper than memory, deeper than words. It is love in its purest form.

Love has carried me, reshaped me, and sustained me. For me, it really is all I need. Love looks different for everyone. For some, it may be the quiet presence of sitting side by side. For others, it may be a shared smile, or even simply showing up when it’s hard. My story is just one thread in the vast tapestry of caregiving, and yours may weave in another way. What I know is that love—in all its forms—has the power to guide us, to soften us, and to remind us of what matters most. May you find your own compass in love, and may it carry you through both the tender and the difficult days.
