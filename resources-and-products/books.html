Books That Have Guided Me on the Caregiving Journey

Throughout my journey as a caregiver, I have found solace, guidance, and inspiration in books. They have helped me understand the complexities of dementia, nurtured my spiritual reflections, and reminded me that I am not alone. Here are some books that have been particularly meaningful to me.

When the Body Says No: The Cost of Hidden Stress

Author: Dr. Gabor Maté

This book helped me see the deep connection between stress, emotional suppression, and physical health. It reminded me that caregiving requires awareness of my own limits and compassion for myself.

The 36-Hour Day

Author: Nancy L. Mace & Peter V. Rabins

A practical guide for caregivers of people with dementia. It offers insight into daily care, communication, and understanding behavioral changes, while also addressing emotional wellbeing for both caregiver and loved one.

Being Mortal: Medicine and What Matters in the End

Author: Atul Gawande

This book explores aging, mortality, and the importance of quality of life. It profoundly influenced how I approach the later stages of my partner’s journey with dementia, focusing on dignity and presence.

The Art of Caring: A Guide for the Soul

Author: Judith Orloff

A deeply compassionate guide that blends practical caregiving advice with spiritual insight. It has helped me honor my feelings while caring for someone I love.
