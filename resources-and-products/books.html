Books That Have Guided Me on the Caregiving Journey

Throughout my journey as a caregiver, I have found solace, guidance, and inspiration in books. They have helped me understand the complexities of dementia, nurtured my spiritual reflections, and reminded me that I am not alone. Here are some books that have been particularly meaningful to me.

When the Body Says No: The Cost of Hidden Stress

Author: Dr. Gabor Maté

This book helped me see the deep connection between stress, emotional suppression, and physical health. It reminded me that caregiving requires awareness of my own limits and compassion for myself.

The 36-Hour Day

Author: Nancy L. Mace & Peter V. Rabins

A practical guide for caregivers of people with dementia. It offers insight into daily care, communication, and understanding behavioral changes, while also addressing emotional wellbeing for both caregiver and loved one.

Being Mortal: Medicine and What Matters in the End

Author: Atul Gawande

This book explores aging, mortality, and the importance of quality of life. It profoundly influenced how I approach the later stages of my partner’s journey with dementia, focusing on dignity and presence.

On Death and Dying: What the Dying have to Teach Doctors, Nurses, Clergy and their own Families

Author: Elizabeth Kübler-Ross

A groundbreaking exploration of the five stages of grief, this book opens a compassionate conversation about death and dying. It offers caregivers deep insight into the emotional experiences of those at the end of life.

Dementia: Living In the Memories of God

Author: John Swinton

This theological reflection challenges society’s view of dementia, affirming the dignity, value, and spiritual presence of those living with memory loss. It offers hope rooted in faith and belonging.

The Grace In Dying: How We Are Transformed Spiritually as We Are Dying

Author: Kathleen Dowling Singh

A deeply spiritual book that describes the dying process as a journey of awakening. Singh shows how death can be a time of grace, transformation, and inner peace for both the dying and their loved ones.

Who Dies: An Investigation of Conscious Living and Conscious Dying

Author: Stephen Levine

This book invites readers to embrace life and death with awareness, compassion, and acceptance. It offers meditations and reflections that help caregivers and loved ones face mortality with courage and love.

The Wheel of Life: A Memoir of Living and Dying

Author: Elizabeth Kübler-Ross

A moving memoir by the pioneer of hospice care, this book reflects on a lifetime of work with the dying. It shares lessons about love, forgiveness, and the beauty of life’s final chapter.

The Veneration of Life: Through the Disease To the Soul

Author: John Diamond, M.D.

An exploration of illness as a pathway to deeper self-understanding and soul connection. Dr. Diamond writes with sensitivity about how disease can reveal profound truths about life and spirit.

Losing Clive to Younger Onset Dementia

Author: Helen Beaumont

A personal and heartfelt memoir of a wife’s journey caring for her husband with early-onset dementia. It offers honest reflections on love, loss, and resilience in the face of a devastating diagnosis.

The Alchemy of Illness

Author: Kat Duff

This lyrical book reframes illness as a transformative experience. Duff blends personal story and philosophy to show how sickness can invite us into greater vulnerability, wisdom, and authenticity.

On Grief and Dying: Understanding the Soul's Journey

Author: Diane Stein

A spiritual perspective on death, grief, and the soul’s transition. Stein offers comfort and insight into the mysteries of dying, helping caregivers find peace in understanding the journey beyond life.

The Majesty of Your Loving: A Couple’s Journey Through Alzheimer's

Author: Olivia Ames Hoblitzelle

A tender and inspiring memoir of a couple navigating Alzheimer’s together. Through mindfulness, love, and acceptance, they find meaning and spiritual growth in the midst of loss and change.
