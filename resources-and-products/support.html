Support and Organizations for Dementia Care

Living with or caring for someone with dementia can be challenging, but you are never alone. There are numerous organizations and support groups that provide guidance, resources, and community for caregivers, family members, and people living with dementia.

International Organizations

  • Alzheimer’s Association (USA) – Provides information, support groups, and resources for families and caregivers.
  • Alzheimer’s Society (UK) – Offers support, factsheets, and local services.
  • Dementia.org – Global dementia resources, advocacy, and research updates.
  • Alzheimer’s Disease International – Worldwide network of dementia organizations, information on awareness campaigns and conferences.
  • https://www.dementiacarecentral.com/ Practical caregiving advice, from daily routines to behavioral strategies.

New Zealand Organizations

  • Alzheimer’s New Zealand – Provides local support groups, helplines, and educational resources.
  • Age Concern NZ – Offers guidance and advocacy for older adults, including those living with dementia.

Support Groups & Online Communities

  • Alzheimer’s Association Community – Online forum for people affected by Alzheimer’s and other dementias. alzconnected.org
  • FTD Support Forum – International forum for Frontotemporal Dementia caregivers and patients. ftdsupportforum.com
  • Dementia Talking Point – UK-based forum for caregivers, family members, and those living with dementia. alzheimers.org.uk
  • Caregiver Action Network – Support forum for caregivers of people with all kinds of chronic illnesses, including dementia. caregiveraction.org/forums
  • Reddit: r/dementia – Community for discussion, advice, and personal stories about dementia. reddit.com/r/dementia
  • Reddit: r/caregivers – Support and resources for caregivers of all types, including those caring for people with dementia. reddit.com/r/caregivers
  • Alzheimer’s Society Forums – UK-based support forums for anyone affected by dementia. alzheimers.org.uk/get-support
  • Family Caregiver Alliance Forum – Forum for family caregivers of people with dementia. caregiver.org/forum

Specialized Support

  • Music & Memory – Uses personalized music playlists to improve quality of life for people with dementia.
  • CareTeams – Platform to coordinate care and communicate with family and support networks.

    • More Resources

    In addition to organizations and support groups, there are many individual caregivers, educators, and practitioners who share their knowledge and experience online. These resources can provide practical tips, demonstrations, and inspiration for your caregiving journey.

    • Teepa Snow – https://teepasnow.com/ Dementia care educator offering videos, workshops, and practical strategies for connecting with people living with dementia.
    • Hospice Nurse Julie – YouTube Channel Shares compassionate advice on end-of-life care, communication, and supporting loved ones through advanced stages of illness.
    • Alzheimer’s Society YouTube – YouTube Channel Videos with tips for everyday care, managing symptoms, and connecting with people living with dementia.

    If you know of additional local or online support groups, please share your links below so others can benefit from the community. Remember, seeking support is a sign of strength, and connecting with others can make this journey more manageable and compassionate.
