How to Make a Fidget Board for a Loved One with Dementia

As dementia progresses, many people develop the need to keep their hands busy. This restlessness is not only natural but can also provide comfort when given a safe outlet. A fidget board (or busy board) is a simple, creative, and inexpensive way to meet this need. It engages the senses, stimulates the mind, and can ease anxiety while bringing a sense of purpose.

What Is a Fidget Board?

A fidget board is a flat surface (usually wood or sturdy cardboard) with a variety of objects attached for someone to touch, turn, click, slide, or explore. It provides tactile stimulation and can remind your loved one of everyday activities in a safe, playful way.

How I Made Mine

For my partner, I started with a piece of wood that I sawed into a square. I then screwed various objects onto it, such as hinges, an old light socket, and even a calculator. I even found a small prayer wheel that could be spun. Each piece gave him something different to fiddle with, and I noticed how calming it was for him to explore with his hands.

Materials You’ll Need

  • A sturdy base – wood, MDF board, or thick cardboard
  • Screws, nails, or strong glue (depending on the objects you’re attaching)
  • A drill or screwdriver
  • Sandpaper (to smooth edges and prevent splinters)

Ideas for What to Add

Here are some creative and safe items you can attach to a fidget board:

  • Hinges and latches – great for moving back and forth
  • Light switches – flipping on and off is soothing and familiar
  • Door handles or knobs
  • Padlocks with keys – if safe and not sharp
  • Calculator or old phone keypad – pressing buttons can be very satisfying
  • Zippers – opening and closing provides rhythmic motion
  • Velcro strips – pulling them apart and pressing together again
  • Old remote control – pressing buttons
  • Soft fabric swatches – different textures for sensory comfort
  • Drawer pulls and handles – for sliding or turning
  • Spinners or wheels – something that rotates
  • Old locks or sliding bolts
  • Beads on a string – to slide back and forth

Safety Tips

  • Make sure all items are securely fastened so they cannot be swallowed or come loose.
  • Sand down any sharp edges.
  • Avoid very small objects, toxic materials, or anything sharp.

Why It Helps

Fidget boards can reduce anxiety, ease agitation, and provide meaningful engagement for your loved one. They allow for repetitive movement, which is calming, and give the hands a sense of “work” that can feel grounding. Best of all, they can be personalized — filled with objects your loved one once used in daily life.

A Creative Gift of Love

Making a fidget board is more than a craft project — it’s a gesture of care. By gathering objects and arranging them thoughtfully, you’re creating not just a tool, but a way to say: I see you, I value you, and I want you to feel safe and engaged. You can buy or custom make a fidget board with meaningful items- the options are endless, let your creativity lead the way!
